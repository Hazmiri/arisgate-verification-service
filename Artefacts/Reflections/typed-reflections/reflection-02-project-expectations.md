# Reflection 02 – Understanding the Project Expectations

## Date
Retrospective reflection written during final submission preparation

## Model
Rolfe et al. – What? So What? Now What?

## What?
At first, I thought the project was mainly about producing working software. Later, after looking at the portfolio checklist and action plan, I understood that the submission is wider than the code. I needed evidence of practice, testing, analysis, synthesis, presentation, and reflection. This made me feel under pressure because I had been focusing heavily on building.

## So What?
This was important because a working prototype alone is not enough if the assessor cannot understand what I built, why I built it, and what I learned. The portfolio has to tell the development story. I started to see the folders, screenshots, test notes, analysis files, README, and reflections as part of the artefact, not as extra decoration.

## Now What?
For future assignments, I will build the portfolio at the same time as the software. I will not leave the evidence until the end. I now understand that professional work needs documentation, testing, and explanation as well as functionality.
