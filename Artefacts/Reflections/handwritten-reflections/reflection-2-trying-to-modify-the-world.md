# Reflection 12 – Trying to Modify the World

## Date
Retrospective reflection based on early project planning

## Model
Rolfe et al. – What? So What? Now What?

## What?

At one point, I realised that I was trying to modify the world with one university project. I wanted ArisGate to connect to Shopify, WooCommerce, Google Maps, WhatsApp, SMS, fraud detection, machine learning, merchant dashboards, admin panels, databases and reports. In my head, it sounded powerful. In reality, it became too much.

I think my lecture would catch this quickly because it is the kind of mistake students make when they have ambition but not enough control. I was not only building a prototype; I was imagining a full company product. That made me feel excited, but it also made the project harder to finish.

## So What?

This mattered because I had to learn the difference between a vision and a submission. The vision can be large, but the artefact must be achievable. I started to understand that a strong project is not the biggest project. It is the project that is controlled, evidenced and explained properly.

This was a professional lesson. In real software teams, nobody builds everything in one sprint. They define the minimum working version, test it, then improve it. I had to apply that thinking to myself.

## Now What?

Now I will describe ArisGate honestly as a proof of concept. I will keep the big vision in the future work section, but I will submit the working backend as the real artefact. Next time, I will write the ambition down, then choose one small slice to build first.
