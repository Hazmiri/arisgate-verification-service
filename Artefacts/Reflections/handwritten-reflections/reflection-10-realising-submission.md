# Reflection 20 – Realising Submission Is a Professional Signal

## Date

27 April 2026

## Model

Rolfe et al. – What? So What? Now What?

## What?

At the end of the project, I started to see the submission differently. Before, I was thinking mainly about the grade. Then I realised that this work is also a signal of the type of professional I am becoming.

The project is not perfect. There are things I would improve, such as real SMS, real geolocation, automated tests and a dashboard. But I also know that I did not give up. I moved from confusion to a working backend, MongoDB records, Postman tests, screenshots, analysis, reflections and a final report.

## So What?

This mattered because I understood that professionalism is not pretending everything is perfect. It is finishing honestly, showing evidence, explaining limitations and learning from mistakes.

I also realised that my journey is not the same as everyone else’s. I am studying, working, learning tools, making mistakes, fixing them and still trying to push the project forward. That process itself is valuable.

## Now What?

After submission, I want to continue improving how I work. I want to become calmer with errors, faster with documentation, stronger with security and more organised from the start. ArisGate taught me that becoming a developer is not one big moment. It is many small moments of not giving up.
