# Reflection 11 – Proposal Submission and the Blank Mind

## Date
Retrospective reflection based on the proposal submission stage

## Model
Rolfe et al. – What? So What? Now What?

## What?

When I submitted the proposal, I remember that I was not feeling confident. I had the idea of ArisGate in my head, but my mind was not organised. I could see the problem of Cash on Delivery fraud and failed orders, but I could not easily turn that into academic writing. I remember sitting there and feeling that the first sentence was the hardest part. It was not the coding that blocked me at that moment; it was starting.

I kept asking myself: what am I actually trying to build? Is this too big? Is it a real project or just an idea? I wanted to explain LBS, OTP, E-commerce, fraud, delivery risk and platform integration all at once. That made the proposal feel heavy.

## So What?

This mattered because it showed me that confusion is not always failure. Sometimes confusion means the project has many parts and my job is to separate them. When I forced myself to write the first idea, the project started to become clearer. I realised that ArisGate did not need to solve the whole world immediately. It needed to show one working verification path.

This was also the first time I understood that writing is part of development. If I cannot explain the system, then maybe I do not fully understand it yet.

## Now What?

In future projects, I will not wait until the idea is perfect before writing. I will start with rough notes, then refine them. I will also break big ideas into smaller questions: what is the problem, who is affected, what can the software prove, and what can wait for later?
