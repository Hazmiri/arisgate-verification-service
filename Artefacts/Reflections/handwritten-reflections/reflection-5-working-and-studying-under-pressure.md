# Reflection 15 – Working and Studying Under Pressure

## Date
Retrospective reflection based on balancing work and study

## Model
Rolfe et al. – What? So What? Now What?

## What?

There were days when I came back from work tired and still had to continue the project. This affected my concentration. Sometimes I knew what I wanted to do, but my brain felt slow. Even simple commands became harder when I was tired.

This made the project feel personal. It was not only a university task in a quiet room. It was something I had to build around real life, work, family, time pressure and fatigue. I remember thinking that other students might have more time, but then I also reminded myself that my life experience is part of my strength.

## So What?

This mattered because I learned that discipline is not only motivation. Motivation comes and goes. Discipline means opening the laptop even when the day has been long and doing one controlled step.

It also made me understand why project management matters. Without small steps, I would have become lost. The commits, folders, test notes and screenshots helped me keep proof of progress even when I could not do everything perfectly.

## Now What?

In future, I will plan my work in smaller blocks. I will not wait for the perfect day. I will aim for one file, one test, one reflection, one commit. Over time, small steps become a full project.
